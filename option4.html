01I'm from a non-journalism background. Can I apply?
Absolutely! ACJ welcomes graduates from all disciplines. The program is designed to train you from scratch — no prior journalism experience needed. Many of our most successful alumni came from engineering, science, and commerce backgrounds.
02Is this a degree or diploma program?
It's a Postgraduate Diploma, not a degree. However, it's recognized by Cardiff University and holds significant value in the media industry. ACJ's reputation and 100% placement record speak to the diploma's real-world credibility.
03What's the difference between Integrated Journalism and Bloomberg Business Journalism?
The Integrated Multimedia Journalism program covers all beats (politics, sports, culture, etc.) and all platforms. The Bloomberg Business & Financial Journalism program specializes exclusively in business reporting. Choose Integrated if you want versatility; choose Bloomberg if you're certain about financial journalism.
04Do you offer scholarships or education loans?
The SAF-Madanjeet Singh Scholarship covers SAARC students fully. ACJ also offers merit-based scholarships. While there are no direct bank loan tie-ups, ACJ's admissions office can advise on current scholarship opportunities and financial aid options.
05What are my chances of getting placed?
ACJ has maintained a 100% placement record historically. The college arranges campus interviews with top media houses, and faculty actively assist students in securing positions. Most graduates find jobs within 3 months of course completion.
06I want to start my own media venture. Is this program for me?
Yes! The curriculum includes an entrepreneurial journalism module specifically designed for aspiring media entrepreneurs. You'll learn to build and monetize your own platform while gaining the foundational skills to sustain yourself through independent journalism.
07Is accommodation mandatory? Where is the campus?
The campus is in Taramani, Chennai. While accommodation isn't technically mandatory, most students stay on-campus since immersive residential life is part of the program's experience. Students from across India and SAARC countries make campus life diverse and enriching.
08Can I pursue the Cardiff University M.A. after this diploma?
Yes, if you meet the benchmark criteria set by Cardiff University. After completing the ACJ diploma, eligible students can transfer credits to Cardiff, complete an additional dissertation, and earn the M.A. in Journalism from Cardiff University — a globally recognized qualification.
09How do I prepare for the entrance test?
The test focuses on English proficiency, general knowledge (especially current affairs), and analytical thinking through an essay. Stay updated with news across politics, economy, sports, and culture. Read quality newspapers daily and practice writing opinion pieces. The interview evaluates your journalistic curiosity and communication skills.
10What if I don't get placed through campus recruitment?
While the placement rate is 100%, in rare cases where students don't secure offers through campus placements, ACJ faculty — many of whom are well-connected industry veterans — continue assisting students. Most find positions within 3 months through faculty referrals and the strong alumni network.
